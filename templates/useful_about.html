Here's the thinking behind this "Useful Thoughts" thingy:

  • Quality of life is a function of moment-to-moment feelings. A good mood makes for an enjoyable life. A chronic bad mood makes for a life of dreary bleakness.

  • Feelings are conditioned by beliefs. If I believe a rope seen in twilight is a snake, I will feel afraid. Believing it to be a harmless rope, I feel no fear of it.

  • Beliefs are established and supported by moment-to-moment thinking.

  • Useful thoughts are those that help us feel good, pursue our aspirations, live out our values, and enjoy our lives.

  • Thoughts that drag us down, make us feel bad, or otherwise impede our enjoyment of life without offering some value (lessons learned, restraint when we want to lash out in anger, acceptance in the face of frustration, etc.) are not useful.

  • Only you can decide which thoughts are useful for you.

  • A thought that is useful today may not be useful tomorrow. A thought that was not useful last week may turn out to be useful today.

  • Because of evolutionary survival pressures, our brains are wired with a negativity bias. As a consequence, some of us may need to make an effort at least some of the time to focus on the positive in our day-to-day routine so that life can be enjoyable.

  • This database is a place to find and suggest useful thoughts. Suggested thoughts will be vetted before being added to the database.

  • Each time you refresh the main page, you'll be presented with half a dozen thoughts randomly selected from the database that someone has found useful. There may be some repetition, but that's okay since thoughts are repetitive by nature. You may not find all the thoughts personally useful. Take what's useful to you and pass the rest by.

    For most of us, our brain's habit is to effortlessly generate a constant stream of negative thoughts. Visiting useful-thoughts.org can help balance the brain's negative tendency with a dose of positive thinking.
